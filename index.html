Dog breeds

The dog (Canis familiaris or Canis lupus familiaris, depending on whether it is considered a species or a subspecies of the wolf), called domestic dog or can, and in some places colloquially called chucho, tuso, choco, among others; It is a carnivorous mammal of the canid family, which constitutes a species of the genus Canis. In 2013, the estimated world population of dogs was between seven hundred million and nine hundred eighty-seven million. Their size (or size), shape and coat are very diverse and vary depending on the breed. It has highly developed hearing and smell, and the latter is its main sensory organ. Their average longevity is ten to thirteen years, depending on the breed. Along with the domestic cat, it is one of the most popular pets in the world.
